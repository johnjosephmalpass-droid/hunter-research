# Pre-freeze research themes surfaced by HUNTER

*Structural themes only. No tickers, no entry prices, no trade directions. Every theme below is a pre-freeze pattern that the summer 2026 pre-registered study is the proper test of, not a confirmed finding. Do not treat this as investment advice or a research note.*

HUNTER produced 324 hypotheses across two pipeline iterations in the pre-freeze window (March 28 – April 3, 2026 for the archive; April 1 – 4 for the main table). Of those, 18 scored diamond ≥ 75 on the internal adversarial review (13 in the archive, 5 in the main table). Those top hypotheses cluster into a handful of structural themes that are worth naming here, not because they are empirically validated (they aren't, yet), but because naming the themes illustrates the kind of cross-silo composition the instrument is built to surface.

---

## 1. Commercial real-estate credit cascade mechanics

Several top-scoring hypotheses share a common structure: a disturbance originates in CMBS credit markets (rising office delinquencies, rating-agency downgrades, stale cap-rate assumptions) and propagates through institutional balance sheets that are methodologically blind to the starting silo. LDI portfolio rebalancing, insurance statutory capital, pension ALM discount-rate construction, and CMBS servicer-reported NOI each process CMBS inputs through their own methodology, creating chains where a change at one end propagates asynchronously through several silos before any single silo fully reprices.

**Why HUNTER keeps surfacing this.** The CMBS → insurance reserve → pension liability → corporate bond curve chain has five distinct silos, each with its own timing convention, and no single professional audience reads all five. That's the exact profile the collision engine is built to detect.

## 2. Municipal and sovereign-adjacent debt with pre-shock underwriting

Multiple high-scoring archive hypotheses concern municipal bonds issued under underwriting assumptions (pro formas, cap rates, environmental cost projections) that were calibrated to a pre-policy-shift regime. Regulatory effective dates, code changes, and infrastructure mandates that post-date the issuance are absent from the issuer's stated assumptions but present in the jurisdiction's regulatory filings.

**Why HUNTER keeps surfacing this.** Municipal underwriting documents are in one silo (financial disclosures); the regulatory changes that make the underwriting stale are in another silo (Federal Register / state regulator). No single professional reads both for the same bond.

## 3. Regulated-utility refinancing under changing FERC authority windows

Independent transmission operators and utility-adjacent issuers face scheduled refinancing windows that coincide with changes to FERC debt authority, rate-structure rules, or interconnection queue economics. The refinancing market they are forced into (commercial mortgage-backed, specialty private credit) does not natively price the regulatory overlap.

**Why HUNTER keeps surfacing this.** Refinancing schedules are in one silo (debt-maturity calendars); FERC rule changes are in another (Federal Register). The overlap is time-boxed and mechanically traceable.

## 4. Pension and insurance discount-rate calibration lag

Pension liability valuations and insurance statutory filings use AA corporate yield curves or similar benchmarks constructed from specific bond universes. When the composition of those universes shifts (rating migrations, issuance waves in adjacent credit classes, CMBS inclusion or exclusion rules), the benchmark moves in a way the downstream actuarial models don't immediately reflect.

**Why HUNTER keeps surfacing this.** Benchmark construction methodology is in one silo (ratings methodology / index construction); actuarial application is in another (ASC 715 / IAS 19 / statutory). The timing of the benchmark shift and its reflection in statutory filings are systematically misaligned.

## 5. Energy forward-curve assumptions about marginal generators

Regional forward power curves (PJM, ERCOT, CAISO adjacent) are priced off assumptions about which generation source is economically marginal at different load levels. When oil-fired peaker economics change (crude price threshold crossings, environmental compliance cost shifts, grid-battery build-out), the marginal-generator assumption breaks, but the forward curve carries the stale assumption until a shock reveals it.

**Why HUNTER keeps surfacing this.** Oil-price dynamics live in one silo (commodity markets); grid-planner forward curves live in another (ISO publications). The bridge, "at what oil price does the peaker no longer clear", is a calculation no single audience performs as a matter of course.

## 6. Hotel-CMBS default-probability models with outdated refinancing-spread assumptions

CMBS tranches backed by hotel properties maturing in 2025–2026 were underwritten with refinancing-spread assumptions from an earlier regime. Current market spreads are materially different, changing default probability inputs without any revision to the pricing models in circulation.

**Why HUNTER keeps surfacing this.** Spread data is in one silo (fixed-income analytics); hotel default modelling is in another (CMBS servicer models). The refresh cycle for the latter is slow.

## 7. Regulatory-capital timing around statutory filing dates

Insurance company regulatory capital is measured at statutory filing dates (Q1 due May 15, Q2 due August 14, etc.). Asset-side valuation movements between filing dates create a timing mismatch: the statutory snapshot captures one moment, but the enforcement consequence (rating actions, regulatory scrutiny) operates on the snapshot. Hypotheses in this cluster identify specific filing cycles where the snapshot-to-consequence lag is exploitable.

**Why HUNTER keeps surfacing this.** Filing-calendar mechanics are in one silo (statutory compliance); asset valuations are in another (capital markets). The mechanical interaction is calendar-driven and therefore predictable.

## 8. Cross-border pharmaceutical demographic assumptions

European pharmaceutical DCF models embedded in equity research use demographic assumptions (population aging, treatment adherence, reimbursement environments) that haven't kept pace with 2024–2026 policy changes in specific jurisdictions. The models are nominally sensitive to demographics but the embedded assumptions are stale.

**Why HUNTER keeps surfacing this.** Demographic projections are in one silo (national statistics / academic); equity-research DCF inputs are in another (sell-side models). The inputs are updated on different cycles.

## What these themes are and aren't

Themes, not findings. No statement above is empirically validated. Each cluster corresponds to one or more pre-freeze hypotheses that scored diamond ≥ 75 under the earlier pipeline, but scoring ≥ 75 under the pre-freeze adversarial reviewer is not the same as having survived a pre-registered out-of-sample test. The summer study is the proper test; until it reports, treat everything here as suggestive, not confirmed.

Compositional, not additive. What all eight themes have in common is that they require reading two or more professional silos simultaneously. Each theme could be dismissed by a specialist in any single one of its silos as "already priced in within my domain", and each of those specialists would be correct about their own silo. The claim is that when you stack the silos, the joint implication is not priced in by anyone because no single specialist holds all the silos.

Structural, not temporal. These are mechanisms, not trades. A mechanism like "statutory filing dates create snapshot-to-consequence timing mismatches" can recur across many specific filings. Pointing at a mechanism is not the same as identifying a specific asset to buy or sell; the specific assets sit in the corpus but are not surfaced here.

## The pre-registered test

The pre-registration manifest (SHA-256 `f39d2f5ff6b3e695`, locked 2026-04-19) commits the summer 2026 run to testing whether the compositional-depth gradient holds across four strata, whether detected cycles persist uncorrected for ≥ 14 days, and whether cross-silo collisions (domain distance ≥ 0.60) score materially higher than within-silo pairs. If the summer produces null results, the paper says so. If it produces supporting results, some subset of the themes above will move from "patterns the instrument keeps surfacing" to "empirically supported under pre-registered conditions."

Neither outcome changes what this document is: a structural cross-section of what HUNTER notices when one person reads 18 professional silos at once.
